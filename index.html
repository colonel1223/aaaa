About

01

I am a 20-year-old researcher based in San Jose, studying economics at SJSU while independently pursuing questions most people don't encounter until graduate school — or ever. My work sits at the convergence of machine learning theory, alignment formalism, and existential risk quantification.

I don't come from a traditional ML pipeline. I didn't wait for permission from a credential. I built simulation environments to study mesa-objective emergence. I wrote conformal prediction frameworks from scratch. I quantified hallucination impossibility across 847,000 traces. The work speaks.

My conviction is simple: this technology will either be the greatest force for human prosperity or the last invention we ever make. I am building the tools to ensure it is the former. Not for status. Not for titles. Because the stakes demand it.

Beyond the research — I am a father, a human being who grew up fascinated by systems and how they break. I taught myself neural network architectures at 18. I read Bostrom before I read my econ textbooks. I think about alignment when most people my age think about other things entirely. This isn't a career move. It's the thing I cannot stop doing.
